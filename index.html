About

Hon. Dr. Doris Uzoka Anite is a career banker with significant experience in Financial Markets and currently heading the Treasury department of one of the biggest banks in Nigeria. She oversees the Fixed Income and Currency Trading desks, Asset and Liability Management, Treasury Corporate Sales, and Financial and Multilateral Institutions. She is responsibility for the investment strategy of the Treasury department and for ensuring adherence to compliance, risk and regulatory requirements of the bank.

Brief History

Born on Oct. 16, Doris is a medical doctor by training and a CFA Charterholder, and currently specializing in Financial Risk Management and Portfolio Management. She made a switch to banking in 2002. She began her career in Banking in and eventually heading the Human Resources and Training dept. She made another switch to Treasury in 2011. Having succeeded at her role in HR, she challenged herself to accomplish the same feat in the Financial Markets. In Treasury, she began with Trading fixed income, then currencies, then heading Treasury sales and finally becoming the Treasurer of the bank. Doris is goal oriented, and customer centric. She is highly analytical as well as being quantitatively inclined. Her excellent performance contributed immensely to the growth of the bank's profitability and this earned her various recognitions and promotions within the organization. Her human resources training inculcated uncommon interpersonal skills in her, a skill which has helped her to manage the diverse local and international relationships that her role demands.

Personal Statement


Throughout her 21-year illustrious career, Hon. Dr. Doris Uzoka Anite has been an ace performer in strategic financial management, driving growth, and stability in both the banking and public sector. The highly coveted Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA) qualification, which she possesses, is considered as the pinnacle of financial achievement globally; and this revered qualification further enhanced her ability to take on very challenging finance issues and proffer rare strategic perspectives and solutions. As Group Treasure of Zenith Bank Plc, the largest bank in Sub-Saharan Africa, she achieved exceptional results, especially during the global financial crises, by deploying advanced Financial Engineering and Financial Risk Management strategies to orchestrate complex financial transactions that propelled the bank towards unparalleled profitability, despite the prevailing economic challenges.

As Group Treasurer, her landmark achievements included:

Balance Sheet Management

Overseeing total assets exceeding $20 billion (N8.48 trillion) for Zenith Bank

Revenue Growth Contribution

Contributing to over 36% growth in the bank's gross revenue.

Investment Advisory

Providing invaluable advice to the executive management and board on investments and policy implications.

Profit Maximisation

Recording an impressive 8% Year-on-Year Trading Profit for FYE 2020, bolstering the bank's financial position through expert Treasury management, cash flow optimization, and prudent debt and liquidity management.

Global Asset and Liability Management

Skillfully managing local and foreign currency asset and liability management for the bank's headquarters and foreign subsidiaries, ensuring financial stability across borders.

In addition to her financial acumen, she understands the significance of human capital as the greatest asset of any organisation or nation. In her role as the Head of HR and Human Capital Development at Zenith Bank, prior to moving to treasury, she recognized the importance of nurturing talents to their fullest potential through precision recruitment, training, mentoring, and coaching. The growth experienced at Zenith Bank, both in substantial balance sheet expansion and in the number of branches opened, attest to the quality of personnel. She has the ability to recognize and nurture talents and she plans to utilise this skill more in Nation building.

More recently, responding to the call to support the development of her beloved home state, Imo State, was more than just a professional endeavour; it was a profound commitment to serving humanity. Driven by an unwavering sense of duty and the pursuit of the greater good, she willingly left her high-profile role as Group Treasurer at Zenith Bank to embark on this paramount mission.
In her capacity as Commissioner for Finance & Coordinating Commissioner of the economy, her professional prowess excelled luminously, leading to a series of landmark achievements that have left a lasting impact on the state's progress and prosperity.

Accomplishments

Outlined below are some of the transformative accomplishments during her tenure, solidly backed up by the Governor, Dis. Senator Hope Uzodimma:

Strategic Financial Management

She prioritised the judicious use of taxpayers' funds, ensuring budgetary compliance, transparency, and accountability. These efforts garnered local and global recognition from esteemed organisations like the World Bank, showcasing the commitment to responsible financial stewardship.

Infrastructural Growth and Financial Stability

Through innovative fiscal and revenue reforms, she facilitated the financing of transformative development and infrastructure projects of Governor Hope Uzodimma, some of which include state-of-the-art roads with lighting of signature roads, rebuilding of run-down and dilapidated buildings such as the Imo House of Assembly complex and the Judges Quarters, etc. Despite facing national economic challenges and heightened insecurity in the region, we managed and grew Imo State's economy and finances by fostering a framework of transparency and responsible fiscal management. As a result, Imo State emerged as the safest and one of the most developed states in the Southeast.

Industry and Trade

A turnaround of the moribund industries of Imo State including the signature Adapalm (The largest oil palm plantation in Nigeria) which is set to achieve profitability and job creation. To crown the economic development effort, Imo State is beginning the dredging of Orashi-Oguta channel to the Atlantic, with the intention of building a seaport to enhance trade and socio-economic development of the region.

Revenue Growth and Resource Maximization

With a keen focus on revenue optimization, she identified revenue sources and potential, effectively addressing revenue retention through talent management and process automation. By diversifying the state's revenue base and reducing dependency on national quota, we strengthened Imo State's fiscal resilience and unleashed its entrepreneurial spirit. This led to the creation of job opportunities for the youth, as we fostered a conducive investment climate and empowered small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) to thrive.

Civil Service Reforms

Payment of all outstanding salaries, even paying 100% Christmas bonuses to all public servants and carried out a double promotion exercise to all civil servants, whose last promotion exercise was 8 years ago.

Treasury and Investment Management

During her tenure, she spearheaded the development of innovative budgetary strategies that optimised public spending, reduced waste, and enhanced efficiency across Ministries, Departments and Agencies. Additionally, she successfully engaged international partners and organisations, leveraging global expertise and resources to prioritise sustainable practices for the state's development.

Policy Advisory

She provided expert insights on investment decisions and their policy implications in aligning financial strategies with the state's long-term goals, further solidifying our commitment to socio-economic development and empowerment.

Youth Empowerment

We conducted IT training and skills development of over 100,000 Imo youths.
